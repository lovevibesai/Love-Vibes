# **MOMENT — TRUST & CONSENT BLUEPRINT**

### *Human Safety as a System, Not a Checkbox*

---

## I. FIRST PRINCIPLE (Anchor)

> **Consent is not a one-time agreement.
> Consent is continuous presence.**

The moment presence is withdrawn, consent is withdrawn.

This single idea governs everything.

---

## II. CORE PHILOSOPHY 

1. **Visibility = Willingness**
2. **Presence = Consent**
3. **Silence ≠ Obligation**
4. **Exit is always allowed**
5. **No human is ever owed attention**

If a system violates any of these, it becomes coercive.

---

## III. CONSENT ARCHITECTURE (LAYERED)

Consent is designed in **layers**, not pop-ups.

---

### LAYER 1: **Intent Consent (Before Entry)**

Before entering any mode, the user must make an **explicit intent choice**:

* “I am open to attraction”
* “I am open to conversation”

This does two things:

* Removes ambiguity
* Prevents expectation mismatch

⚠️ **No default mode**
Choosing nothing = not visible.

---

### LAYER 2: **Presence Consent (Real-Time)**

Consent exists **only while**:

* User is active
* User is present
* User has not exited

The moment the user:

* Switches apps
* Locks screen
* Turns off camera/mic

→ The system **immediately notify then disconnects**.

No lingering access.
No background consent.

---

### LAYER 3: **Mutuality Consent**

For **any interaction to continue**, consent must be:

* Mutual
* Explicit
* Simultaneous

Examples:

* Both choose “Stay”
* Both continue speaking
* Both remain present

One-sided interest has **no mechanical power**.

---

### LAYER 4: **Escalation Consent**

Escalation is never automatic.

Each step requires **active choice**:

* Silent video → Text
* Text → Voice
* Voice → Video

No auto-unlocking features.

This protects:

* Shy users
* Vulnerable users
* Power imbalance scenarios

---

## IV. DISCLAIMER DESIGN (HUMAN, NOT LEGAL)

Disclaimers are not walls of text.
They are **truth statements**, delivered contextually.

---

### DISCLAIMER 1: **Emotional Reality Disclaimer**

Displayed on first use (once, clearly):

> “This platform makes real possible and valueble outcomes.
> It creates moments of real human presence.”

Purpose:

* Removes false hope
* Reduces emotional dependency
* Protects mental health

---

### DISCLAIMER 2: **No Obligation Disclaimer**

Before first interaction:

> “You are never required to continue a moment.
> Leaving is always acceptable.”

This **normalizes exit**, reducing guilt-based compliance.
stll you never know the same person might come across

---

### DISCLAIMER 3: **No Recording Disclaimer**

Before camera/mic activation:

> “Recording, saving, or sharing moments is prohibited.”

This sets:

* Psychological safety
* Behavioral boundaries
* Legal protection

---

## V. VIOLATION MECHANICS (CRITICAL)

Violations are handled through **behavioral thresholds**, not reports alone.

---

### CATEGORY A: **Hard Violations (Zero Tolerance)**

Immediate action, no warnings:

* Recording or screenshots
* Sexual exposure
* Harassment or threats
* Hate speech
* Repeated boundary pushing after refusal

**Response:**

* Instant session termination
* Immediate account suspension
* Evidence preservation (server-side)
* Human review before reinstatement (if any)

---

### CATEGORY B: **Soft Violations (Pattern-Based)**

Examples:

* Ignoring exits
* Pressuring for escalation
* Repeated uncomfortable behavior
* Manipulative language

**Response:**

* Silent friction added (reduced visibility)
* Warning delivered in neutral tone
* Escalation leads to suspension

Key idea:

> The system corrects behavior *before* punishment.

---

### CATEGORY C: **Consent Drift (Unique to MOMENT)**

This is important and often ignored.

Consent drift occurs when:

* One user disengages emotionally
* Silence becomes discomfort
* Energy drops

The system detects:

* Long pauses
* Repeated non-responses
* Abrupt exits

**Response:**

* Moment gently gives popup notification
*
* Ssession terminats

This protects emotional dignity.

---

## VI. REPORTING MECHANISM (NON-TRAUMATIC)

Reporting must not:

* Re-traumatize
* Force explanation
* Demand emotional labor

Design rules:

* One-tap reporting
* No required narrative
* Optional voice/text add-on
* Clear confirmation of action taken

---

## VII. POWER BALANCE PROTECTION

### Visibility Equality

* No one can dominate attention
* No “celebrity stacking”
* Exposure limits apply to all

### Gender & Vulnerability Awareness

* Escalation steps slow down for:

  * New users
  * Repeatedly reported users
* Vulnerable users get **priority exit mechanics**

---

## VIII. CHILD & AGE SAFETY (MANDATORY)

* Hard age verification (not self-declared)
* No cross-age visibility
* Immediate ban for circumvention attempts

No exceptions.

---

## IX. THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE (WRITE THIS IN STONE)

> **A moment ends the second it stops feeling safe.**

The system must always side with:

* The one leaving
* The one uncomfortable
* The one silent

---

## X. WHY THIS BLUEPRINT IS ESSENTIAL TO MOMENT

Because:

* Real-time presence is powerful

> It optimizes for dignity.

That is why it will survive regulation, scale ethically, and earn trust.
